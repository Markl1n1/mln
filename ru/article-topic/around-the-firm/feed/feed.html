]]> The MLPride Network recently hosted a volunteer event at our NYC headquarters in support of the Brooklyn Community Pride Center’s Pride Pantry.

The Brooklyn Community Pride Center is committed to supporting one of the largest and most diverse LGBTQ+ communities in the U.S. through socially-conscious and culturally-responsive programming and advocacy work in NYC. The Pride Pantry, located at the center’s facility in Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brooklyn, offers essential items to community members facing homelessness and housing insecurity.

Volunteers prepared care kits and snack packs with hygiene essentials and non-perishable foods for the center’s Pride Pantry.

During the event, volunteers were also able to learn about the center’s mission, partnerships and events in an information session led by Omari Scott, Brooklyn Community Pride Center Director of Development & Communications.

]]> A number of events were held across the firm in celebration of International Women’s Day. Learn more about the events across our global offices below. 

Americas

In the Americas, WOMN, WIN and WIT hosted two conversations at our New York headquarters on Wednesday, March 6th.

The first conversation featured Adena T. Friedman, Nasdaq CEO and Chair, and was moderated by Stacy Selig, our Deputy COO. Adena discussed Nasdaq’s priorities, her career journey and the importance of company culture.

In the evening, the networks held a fireside chat with Millennium’s Julia Raiskin, CEO, APAC, which was moderated by our Chief Communications Officer, Rosanna Konarzewski. Julia discussed Millennium’s presence in the APAC region, her experience building a career in Asia and what excites her about markets and finance.

 APAC

In Singapore and Hong Kong, WOMN co-hosted events with Citi’s Women’s Committee. Both offices participated in a studio cooking class and socialized over a meal together afterwards.

EMEA

In EMEA, WOMN held a fireside chat with Camila Penna, J.P. Morgan Head of EMEA and LATAM Cash Equities Sales and Execution Services Sales, moderated by Daniel Mallinson, WOMN EMEA Sponsor and Head of Execution Services, EMEA. Camila reflected on her career journey from Brazil to London, discussed the representation of women in the finance industry, and shared her personal experience with balancing a family and career.

]]> Families at Millennium (FAM) is the firm’s newest employee network and is designed to support employees through all stages of caregiving, from current to future caregivers. FAM aims to create a culture of balance between work and family life through cultivating a community, increasing awareness of firm policies and resources and providing content through speakers, workshops, networking events and more.

Some of FAM’s recent events have included:

FAM hosted a First Aid CPR & AED training course led by Shane Woodall, Founder and President of Frontline Health. The in-person and virtual course covered how to perform CPR, use an automated external defibrillator (AED) device and provide choking relief for adults and children.

FAM invited employees in the New York office to join a cooking class to learn recipes that are easy and fun to make with kids. A Registered Dietician led the class and taught the group healthy recipes. On the menu: sushi rolls and salad in a jar.

FAM joined with Millennium’s Multicultural Network to host a joint workshop focused on Raising Empathetic and Inclusive Children. The facilitator shared resources on how to raise compassionate children in a changing world, with a focus on combating racism, ableism and genderism.

FAM hosted a Networking Event so that caregivers could socialize with colleagues and learn more about the network and its initiatives.

]]> The Millennium Multicultural Network (MCN) brings together colleagues from across Millennium to further understand race, culture and ethnicity. Through a variety of events, MCN provides a community grounded in education and connection at Millennium

Some of MCN’s recent events have included:

MCN held a Hispanic Heritage Month networking event last September. The evening included conversation over a sommelier-guided tasting of wines from Hispanic regions.

MCN joined with the Families of Millennium Network to host a joint workshop focused on Raising Empathetic and Inclusive Children. The facilitator shared resources on how to raise compassionate children in a changing world, with a focus on combating racism, ableism and genderism.

MCN partnered with Apex for Youth to host a virtual story time event. Employees read children’s books focused on the Lunar New Year.

To celebrate Black History Month, MCN hosted an immersive 360° national virtual tour traversing from the South to the North, delving into the profound and multifaceted history of African Americans in America. This tour celebrated the enduring legacy of African American heritage, unveiling the impactful stories of artists, entrepreneurs, writers and activists who have been instrumental in shaping the nation’s history.

The office has space for our growing presence in Israel. Millennium has been steadily building its presence in Israel since February 2019, when Millennium first launched its Quantitative Modeling group in the region. In addition to the Quantitative Modeling and Technology group, the Tel Aviv office has employees representing Risk Technology, Treasury Technology and GCP Engineering and Enablement.

To celebrate the opening, employees went on a culinary tour in the Levinsky market, tasting different foods and learning about the historical significance of different cultures that have immigrated to Israel.
